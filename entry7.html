Entry 7: Over the Moon


Over the Moon

To celebrate finishing a week of 3 midterms, I wanted the Netflix movie Over the Moon with my mother. I just wanted a movie to watch, so I picked this movie at random, but little did I know that it’d be a beautiful tale of a Chinese-American girl building a rocket to meet the moon goddess Chang’e. Chang’e was the wife of Houyi, who was the archer who shot down nine of the ten suns. She drank an elixir that made her immortal, so she floated to the moon, waiting for Houyi to eventually join her.

The art and music and animation were absolutely perfect, and I appreciated how not only was the entire voice cast Asian-American, but the production, design, and animation teams included several Asian people too. In the behind the scenes, the head animator was shown to have visited China and consulted and collaborated with Chinese animators and designers. This brought so much more authenticity and representation to the movie. Not only did I love the representation, but I also loved the message that the movie embodied. The protagonist and Chang’e helped each other learn that though love is for eternity, they’re allowed to move on from someone’s passing and begin loving other people. It was a beautiful story of healing. The drawing above is of Chang’e meeting her husband again while he tells her that she must move on before disappearing again.
