Spatial Analysis - Composer's Memo

Where I describe the layout and design of my living room.

Composer's Memo

During an era without COVID, I often introduced my friends who had never been to my house to my living room first. When reading the assignment prompt, I wanted to replicate this experience because I view my classmates as friends who haven’t been to my house yet. However, I couldn’t meet my classmates in person and show them my house. Taking inspiration from my quarantine hobby of watching Youtube videos, I decided that if I couldn’t meet people in person, then I’d meet them through a video. I tried recording a tour of my living room, acting how I usually do when I lead my friends on a house tour. I believed that a tour video would be the most effective medium to convey the necessary information related to my living room because the viewer can see exactly what in the living room I’m referring to and get additional angles. The video is linked from the screenshot of a fake video, so if the image is clicked, then the link to the living room tour appears.

I faced several challenges choosing a video route, though. First, I struggled to find moments when my mother would be out of the house, for I wanted certainty of no interruptions while not preventing my mother from doing her busy work. Initially, I decided to solve this problem by coming up with a transcript of a fake video of me touring my living room, but I finally found a moment when I could record in an empty house. Another challenge I needed to overcome was my speaking abilities. I had to do several takes due to stumbling over my words, but I realized that I typically stumble, so if I were to give an in-person tour with friends, that’s how I’d speak. I didn’t want to sound too different from my natural self and accepted that I’d never get a perfect take.
